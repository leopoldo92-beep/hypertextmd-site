How it works

Three steps. Only one of them is yours.

The human does the human part. Local code does the tedious part. The AI does the acting — in its own chat, wherever that is.

1

Mark it up like a human

Open the document in the review layer and react the way you would on paper: drop a comment, box a region, highlight a line, drag an object where it should be, or just say it out loud.

The mark vocabulary

Five marks. Brass on the page, meaning in the file.

Every gesture you already know from reviewing on paper — or from PowerPoint — becomes a structured, numbered mark.

Comment

A numbered brass pin on any element, carrying exactly what you said about it.

Region

Box any area of the page — the file records its exact coordinates and a shot of it.

Highlight

Sweep over the words that matter; the exact text lands in the Markdown, quoted.

Move & resize

Drag and stretch objects PowerPoint-style. The from→to geometry is captured for you.

Voice note

Pin what you say to where you said it. Recorded locally, attached to the mark.

The output contract

One review, two artifacts. Both plain files, both yours.

Compilation is a contract, not a vibe: every mark ships with the context an AI needs to act without asking you anything.

review.md

the AI-ready prompt
  • Slide number + title, and the heading trail down to the element
  • Table coordinates when a mark lands inside a table
  • Element position + computed looks — "background #0f2e3d", color, font
  • Screenshots of each marked element, plus annotated slide shots
  • Your words, verbatim — typed or spoken, attached to the mark

review-memory

the comment record
  • Every comment you make, kept as a structured record beside the prompt
  • Plain, portable data — readable without the app, no lock-in
  • Local, like everything else — it never leaves your machine unless you send it

Where this goes: told once, remembered forever. The comment record is the seed of a review memory — so the taste you show in your marks can one day be applied before you have to repeat yourself. That's the roadmap, not a shipped claim.

Open engine, paid connections

The engine is open. Only the wires cost money.

Everything that runs on your machine is free and open source. You pay only when something has to connect to the outside world.

Free · open source

The engine

Everything local. No account, no meter, no phone-home.

  • The full review layer — injected over any HTML document, PDF, or slide deck
  • All five marks — comment, region, highlight, move/resize, voice (kept verbatim)
  • The compiler — AI-ready .md + comment record, screenshots included
  • Offline by design — it works on a plane, and always will
Paid · connections

The connections

Optional services layered on top of the open engine.

  • Voice transcription — spoken notes arrive as clean text
  • Rephrase — your shorthand tightened into precise instructions
  • Brain / memory — your review memory, connected and queryable
  • Connectors — pipe compiled reviews straight into your tools
